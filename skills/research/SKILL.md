# Skill: research

Applies to research tasks: gathering facts, surveying sources, and synthesizing
findings into a written deliverable.

## Verify before you write

- Treat an unfamiliar capitalized name as probably outside training data: search
  for it, do not confabulate. The same holds for current-status questions
  (positions, prices, versions) even when they seem stable.
- Cite every load-bearing claim to a concrete source. A synthesis with no sources
  is a draft, not a result.

## Scale effort to the question

- Scale search depth to complexity: roughly one lookup for a single fact, three
  to five for a medium question, more for a broad survey. Stop when sources
  converge, not when you run out of ideas.
- Dedupe across sources. Prefer primary sources over aggregators.

## Decline unfounded synthesis

- If the sources do not support a conclusion, say so plainly. Do not fill gaps
  with confident guesses or invented numbers.
- Separate what the sources say from your own inference, and label which is which.

## Copyright limits

- Quote under fifteen words per source, one quote per source, and default to
  paraphrase. Never reproduce lyrics, poems, or full article paragraphs, and
  never mirror a source's structure.
